Preface

About the Authors

Drs. Caspar & Melchior

Recognized as the foremost experts on the Riktasthani-yuga and its chief polity, the Suryagarh Empire. Their centuries of careful study of historical records have yielded a wealth of understanding about the Suryagarhan empire, the Asuras who ruled over it, and the social upheavals of the era.

Dr. Balthazar

His excavations of important Suryagarhan archaeological sites have provided direct evidence of many theories, granting us the ability to directly examine Swargalokan history rather than merely conjecture about it. Together, their body of work has informed over eleven civilizations' perceptions of the Riktasthani-yuga.

I
The Major Players

The Dominant Polity

The Suryagarh Empire

Political Identity

Suryagarh

The leading polity during the Riktasthani-Yuga, composed of many different species but ruled over by the Devas. Principal cities included the capital, Amaravati, and the port city of Tarakamaya.

I

Historical Legacy

The Devas' Empire

Waged a prolonged war on the Asuras that ended when Emperor Suryavaan sealed away their leadership. The current polities of Swargaloka ultimately descend from the members of its ruling class.

II

The Opposition

The Asuras

Our research uncovered that the Asuras came from a dimension beyond our own, in which they had been sealed long ago by unknown forces. Their initial entry into Swargaloka was dictated by a need to re-assert their existence in the face of a prolonged, potentially permanent dormancy.

Antagonist — Suryagarh

Vikarna

Of unknown origins, Vikarna united the disparate Asura tribes after a protracted struggle with rival chieftains. He became the most feared general Swargaloka had ever seen.

V

Emperor — Suryagarh

Suryavaan

Born in RY 143879. Crowned Emperor after the death of his father in RY 145326. Led the Suryagarhan Empire for millennia, bringing it into a new age of prosperity — and ultimately sealing away Vikarna at Mt. Meru.

III

Unknown Allies

The Three Wise Men

Among Vikarna's personal effects we discovered journals written by a group calling themselves the "Three Wise Men". We believe these three were close confidants of Vikarna; their journal speaks extensively of having interacted with him and helped manage his personal affairs.

Interestingly, nowhere else in Swargalokan history have these "Three Wise Men" been recorded — leading us to believe they went to extraordinary lengths to conceal their identities.

— Caspar & Melchior, preliminary notes

II
The Asura-Suryagarha War

"History is not decided by the truth-tellers, but rather by the victors."

— Journal no. 3, Entry 18/45/15371, "M."

Causes of Conflict

Why and How?

Stelai excavated near New Amaravati indicate that Vikarna's proclamation of war against the Suryagarhan Empire arose from his desire to alter Swargaloka's Akashic Records. The Devas, viewing this as an outright threat to their continued primacy, mobilized the different races of Swargaloka against the Asuran threat.

500 years of war ensued — leading to millions of lives lost on both sides and countless cities destroyed.

Chronology

The War Timeline

Declaration of War

Suryavaan declares war on the Asuras following Vikarna's proclamation.

RY 14873

Surya Stambh Offensive

Asuras begin their advance on major Suryagarhan cities.

RY 14921–15121

200-Year Stalemate

Heavy attrition of forces along front lines on both sides. Neither army can break through.

RY 15122

Rise of General Veer Arka

His tactical genius renewed the Suryagarhan army and allowed them to truly succeed against the Asuras for the first time.

RY 15272

Battle of Nirvaan Kshetra

Asura forces unable to mount even token resistance — a turning point in Suryagarhan history. Victory achieved swiftly, at great civilian cost.

RY 15372

Battle of Mt. Meru

The final battle. Suryavaan seals away Vikarna in Patala. Without their leader, the Asura armies surrender immediately. End of the Asura Wars.

Source: M. Balthazar, Vikarna: Life & Times, pp. 236–238 · Annals of Suryagarh, pp. 3456–3469

The Official Record

The Traditional Account

Vikarna's exact motives were never determined, and no one knows what became of him after his sealing. The Suryagarh Empire went on to last additional centuries before splitting apart into numerous daughter polities around RY 15813. The remaining Asuras either fled Swargaloka or slowly integrated into Suryagarhan society.

⬥ Previously Undisclosed Research ⬥

The Missing Piece

Anara

Our ground-breaking research revealed that prior to engaging Suryagarh in open warfare, Vikarna had a Deva consort named Anara. What few surviving accounts we could find of her suggested she was both beautiful and kind, free of the usual prejudices against Asuras.

In traditional historiography, she is sometimes mentioned only as a "corrupting influence" on middle period Suryagarh — a deliberate erasure of her true significance.

For the "crime" of associating with an Asura, Anara was personally sentenced to "erasure" by Emperor Suryavaan — an unprecedented direct alteration to Swargaloka's Akashic Records.

— Exclusive archive discovery, New Amaravati

The Descendants

Did They Survive?

Evidence suggests Anara and Vikarna had at least two children — essentially unrecorded in any piece of official Swargalokan history. The last entry in the journal of "C." talks about having "hidden away two children… their mother taken away from them… their father an enemy of the world… far away from the eyes of Swargaloka."

This suggests that the Three Wise Men helped ensure the continuation of Vikarna's legacy — not just historically, but physically as well.
